Wind Speed

The winds at all latitudes to the north of 0Â° deflect to the right of their intended path in the Northern Hemisphere. If the earth did not rotate, an object would continue on a straight-line path without appearing to deflect at all. The amount of deflection varies with latitude, the speed of the object and the rotation of the earth. The deflection is greatest at the poles and decreases to zero at the equator.
